# Concepts

The distributed-systems ideas the build keeps shoving in your face — one page each, explained the way a friend would over coffee, with a diagram instead of a wall of text wherever one earns its place.

Phase 0 (foundations) stood up the infrastructure substrate, so these first few are about _running Kafka and its neighbours_ — keeping them alive, not yet pushing events through them. The juicier stuff (partitions, consumer groups, the log itself) lands as the services that need it get built.

- **[KRaft mode](/concepts/kraft-mode)** — Kafka without ZooKeeper; how a single broker elects itself controller, and why that takes a moment. _Interactive._
- **[Healthchecks](/concepts/healthchecks)** — what "healthy" means in Compose, and the `start_period` that keeps a slow-booting service from flapping.
- **[Auto topic creation](/concepts/auto-topic-creation)** — why your first `kcat` produce just works locally, and why that same convenience is a footgun in production.
- **[Named volumes](/concepts/named-volumes)** — how Postgres keeps its data across `docker compose down`, and what a named volume actually is.
